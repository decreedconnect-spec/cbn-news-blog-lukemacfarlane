Luke Macfarlane
Graces Vogue Slovenija
April 2026 Cover

The Canadian-American actor and LGBTQ+ icon becomes the first North American male celebrity to front the cover of Slovenia's premier fashion magazine — shot on location in Ljubljana's historic Old Town.

Full Coverage

Luke Macfarlane has long been celebrated for his performances on screen, but his April 2026 appearance on the cover of Vogue Slovenija marks an extraordinary new milestone in his already distinguished career. Photographed in the golden light of Ljubljana's cobblestoned Old Town, the Canadian-born actor radiates the kind of effortless, lived-in confidence that has made him one of Hollywood's most compelling presences.

The cover, lensed by renowned European fashion photographer Matej Leskovar, features Macfarlane in a series of looks that move fluidly between relaxed elegance and quiet power — a cream linen blazer against whitewashed limestone walls; a tailored charcoal overcoat in the shadow of Ljubljana Castle. The editorial team at Vogue Slovenija described the shoot as "a meditation on identity, belonging, and the beauty of being seen."

"There's something deeply moving about being invited into a country's culture through its fashion. Slovenia surprised me in every possible way — the architecture, the people, the light. It felt like coming home to a place I'd never been."
— Luke Macfarlane, Vogue Slovenija, April 2026

A Historic First for the Publication

Vogue Slovenija, which launched in 2017 under the Condé Nast umbrella, has steadily built a reputation for bold, culturally engaged covers that reflect both European sophistication and a distinctly Slovenian perspective. Macfarlane's appearance is the first time a North American male celebrity has graced the publication's cover, and editors say it reflects a deliberate editorial shift toward global storytelling.

"Luke embodies a kind of masculine grace that is rarely celebrated in mainstream culture," said Editor-in-Chief Polona Kovač in a statement accompanying the issue. "He is an artist, an advocate, and a genuinely kind human being. We are honored to have him as the face of our April issue."

LGBTQ+ Advocacy Remains Central to His Identity

Since coming out publicly in 2008, Macfarlane has been an unapologetic advocate for LGBTQ+ visibility in Hollywood. His starring role alongside Billy Eichner in the 2022 Universal Pictures romantic comedy Bros — the first major studio film centered on a gay romance — earned him widespread critical praise and cemented his status as a trailblazer in the industry. A sequel, confirmed by Eichner to be in early development, is expected to resume production later this year.

The timing of the Vogue Slovenija cover aligns with a particularly active awards season for LGBTQ+ storytelling. Macfarlane is set to receive the Vito Russo Award at next month's GLAAD Media Awards — an honor given to out media professionals who have made a significant difference in promoting equality. Previous recipients include Jodie Foster, Charlize Theron, and Laverne Cox.

What's Next: Amazon Prime and Beyond

Beyond the awards circuit, Macfarlane's professional slate continues to expand at a remarkable pace. CBN can confirm that he is attached to star in The Long Way Round, a new limited series for Amazon Prime Video described by insiders as "a deeply personal road drama with an emotionally devastating final act." Production is scheduled to begin in British Columbia this summer.

Time Magazine's forthcoming LGBTQ+ Trailblazers list — an annual celebration of 100 figures shaping the culture — will also feature Macfarlane prominently. The full list is expected to be published in mid-April 2026.
